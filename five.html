Dipolar Disorder

OCD

Bipolar disorder is sometimes regarded as a disorder of no significance when compared to the main illnesses such as depression and anxiety. However, it is an extreme illness that consists of extreme mood swings , including manic (high energy) and depressive (low energy) episodes. These swings in one's feelings can greatly affect the completion of daily activities.

Common Signs:

  1. Manic Episodes: Common Signs of Manic episodes include elevated mood or irritability. This is the part of the mood swing where people feel lots of energy and begin to take dangerous risks as well. Rapid speech and racing thoughts are common symptoms of people being in such episodes.
  2. Depressive Episodes: This can be thought of the opposite of Manic Episodes, or the low part of the mood swing. These episodes almost always have extremely depressing thoughts, and low energy which impedes daily activities. Some other signs of these episodes include inability ot concentrate, and sometimes even drastic thoughts about death and/or suicide.

Causes: Causes of bipolar disorder are a multitude of other causes described in previous pages.

  1. Genetics And Brain structure: A strong family history of bipolar disorder or other mood disorders may increase the chance of yourself suffering from one of these disorders. In addition, certain abnormalities in brain areas that regulate mood and behavior can lead to the sudden mood swings so often found in patients with bipolar disorder.
  2. Environmental Stressors: Some sudden events in your life that have extreme consequences on your daily activities may lead to the experience of one of the two episodes listed above.

Combat (treatment):

  1. Psychotherapy and Medication: Of course, different types of medication can be prescribed to reduce the effects of bipolar disorder. This includes mood stabilizers (ex: lithium), antipsychotics (ex: quetiapine), and antidepressants. Medication adherence is crucial for managing symptoms that otherwise would have significant impacts on your lifestyle. As always, CBT and psychoeducation help individuals understand their condition, recognize early signs of mood changes, and develop coping strategies.
  2. Lifestyle Changes: To better combat the symptoms of bipolar disorder, a better management of sleep and avoidance of drugs is recommended. In addition, routine medical check ups are beneficial along with check ins with family members (used as a support group in some cases)
