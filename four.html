Eating disorders are serious conditions related to persistent eating behaviors that negatively impact health, emotions, and the ability to function in important areas of life. Common types include anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder. There is a common myth surrounding eating disorders that it only deals with obesity or extreme anorexia, but there are many other disorders that people suffer from.

There are multiple sings dealing with the consumption of food that lead to certain eating disorders such as the following:

  1. Anorexia Nervosa: Common Signs of Anorexia is when people start to become afraid of the intake of food so much, to the point where it leads to significant weight loss and a distorted body image. Many factors lead to this sense of fear, but symptoms include neglecting the severity of not eating the right amount of food.
  2. Bulimia Nervosa: This disorder is usually found in people who start to experience harmful effects due to recurrent binge eating. During these binge episodes people may vomit right after eating or feel a sense of lack of control whilst in the middle of an episode.
  3. Binge-Eating Disorder: Similarly to Bulimia Nervosa, common symptoms include finding yourself eating large amounts of food in a very short period of time. Resulting symptoms include feelings of intense discomfort, shame, and guilt due to the intake of such a large amount of food.

Multiple factors lead to people starting to find themselves in the middle of an eating disorder. The factors are a combination of the above 4 disorders previously mentioned.

  1. Genetics: Explained before
  2. Traumatic Experiences: A common cause is bullying of one's bodily image.
  3. Sociocultural Influences: Living in a certain culture where a specific body image is hailed as being glorious, even if it is naturally unattainable

Combat (Treatment)

  1. A combination of previous treatments such a Psychotherapy and Support groups.
  2. Medication: Different medication aids in the suppression of these eating disorders when compared to previous disorders. This includes antidepressants that may be prescribed to treat co-occurring conditions like depression or anxiety.
  3. Nutritional Counseling: Provides education on balanced nutrition and helps restore healthy eating patterns. This counseling may aid peoples journey back to normalization with regards to their diet. It will also help them realize what is wrong with their diet, and the necessary steps to fix it.
