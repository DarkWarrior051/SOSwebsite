OCD

OCD is characterized by unwanted, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) performed to alleviate the distress caused by these thoughts. It can severely impact daily life and functioning. OCD is another disorder that is glossed over by many people, and its significance is not truly understood.

Common Signs: These symptoms may be found in daily activities and feelings that some people start to experience.

  1. Obsession: Common symptoms of OCD include recurring feelings of obsession over daily activities. Some people tend to obsess over the execution of some activities for example, or the immense feeling that a certain object needs to be in symmetrical order. Any of these thoughts of urges may cause extreme anxiety if not done correctly.
  2. Compulsion: Compulsions can be thought of as certain activities that are done in order to rescue one's anxiety. This includes examples such as washing your hands a specific way or even arranging things in a certain order.
  3. Impact: If you start to realize that you experience one of the symptoms above, try and figure out whether or not these symptoms start to have significant impacts on your social or physician lifestyle.

Causes:

  1. Genetics, brain, Environment: Same as above disorders, although some differences in brain areas related to behavior regulation, such as the orbitofrontal cortex and basal ganglia are associated with OCD.

Combat (treatment):

  1. Medication: There are different types of medicine available to people experiencing OCD and its harmful symptoms. SSRIs (ex: fluoxetine, sertraline) are commonly prescribed to manage symptoms.
  2. Psychotherapy: A common theme among these disorders is the treatment dealing with psychotherapy. As mentioned above, and related to OCD, CBT, particularly exposure and response prevention (ERP), is effective in reducing symptoms. ERP involves gradually exposing individuals to anxiety-provoking situations and preventing the compulsive response. This gradual revelation will help people build a response to these situations, hoping that they will not feel any sort of anxiety after building up a feeling of immunity to these situations.
